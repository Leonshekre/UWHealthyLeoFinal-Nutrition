Nutrition

College students around the ages of 18 usually need 2200 calories (for women) and 2500- 3000 calories (for men) every day. A person’s calorie necessities largely depend on the person’s age, build, activity level, and other variables, so the “average” calorie intake should be verified by a nutritionist or doctor if possible.

The 3 musketeers of nutrients: Proteins, Carbohydrates, and Fat

Carbs and Fat shouldn’t be avoided at all, in fact half of one’s calories comes from Carbs alone! There are good and bad sources of fat and carbohydrates, and this misconception that all of them should be avoided is very harmful.

The 3 main types of carbs are sugars, fibers, and starches and these can either be complex or simple. Usually complex carbs are considered “healthier” because they usually take longer to break down as well as having more important nutrients for the body. Fats also have a misconception, monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats are usually healthy while saturated fats are unhealthy.

Vitamins and Minerals

A critical part of one's diet that many neglect

The 3 main vitamins are A,D, and E.
These vitamnis help with eyesight and the immune system; calcium usage; and the immune system respectively

The main minerals include: Calcium, Folate, Iron, Zinc, and Magnesium

Calcium: bone strength|Folate + zinc: growth|Iron: red blood cell production|Magnesium: heart health and bone strength

Of course, there are more than just the 3 main vitamins such as Vitmain K which helps with healing. Fiber may not be considered a vitamin or mineral but it is still an important nutrient that promotes weight loss and lowers sugar levels

Sugar

According to the Association for Health Alternatives Males should consume no more than 36 grams of sugar while women should consumer less than 24 grams of sugar... yet

The average sugar intake for people in the United States is an astonishing...

71 grams of sugar

That is double what is necessary to be healthy!

Nutrition in foods!

Nutrients in...

Rice!

Rice is a good food source that can be filling and healthy if mixed with the right ingredients

Brown rice and White rice are both abundant in carbs and calories, but brown rice has "good carbs"... yes those exist! White rice undergoes a process that strips it of its fibers, vitamins, and minerals so it isn't as nutritious as brown rice, although it is still an option if paired with the right ingredients

Brown rice is very nutrient rich, it contains:
Fiber
Antioxidants
Vitamins
Minerals

Superfoods!

Lentils

One of my favorite super foods. Lentils aren't that popular but they provide an incredible source of nutrients.
Lentils have B vitamins, magnesium zinc, potassium, iron, and are 25% protien! They provide so many benefits that I cannot recommend this enough (especially since its so delicious!)


Quinoa

This superfood has even more benefits, in fact it was called the "mother grain" by the Inca.
Quinoa can be considered a rice substitute (mostly white rice) that provides incredible sources of fiber, protien, B vitamins, magnesium, and iron as well as being gluten-free!
Quinoa 15% protein while also being low-fat. The amount of nutrients in this food is too good to pass up on



Salads

The ingredients in salads...

    Lettuce
    Lettuce can provide some nutrients, however "Iceberg" lettuce is at the bottom of the list when it comes to nutrients. The best alternative is Romaine lettuce which provides a decent amount of Vitamin A, potassium and calcium.
    Spinach
    Spinach provides a much greater assortment of nutrients including iron, calcium, and magnesium. The best reason to eat spinach is its antioxidant which is very good at managing diabetes
    Broccoli
    Surprisingly Broccoli has as much Vitamin C as an orange! It has vitamins and minerals such as: calcium, iron, potassium, zinc, folate, vitamin A, D, and E. Just like spinach, brocolli helps with diabetes.
    Tomatoes
    Although it may be considered a "fruit" it is often thought of as a vegetable, one that has abundant sources of Vitamin C, Potassium, and Folate
    Cucumbers
    These vegetables are about 95% water so they keep one hydrate. They are also a good source of fiber, Vitamin K and Vitamin A
